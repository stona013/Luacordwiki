Description, Flavor & Backstory

Nox are humanoid, bat-like creatures with long extremities and big, leathery wings. Though flightless due to their size, their leathery wings are thought to be a symbol of status

Race Names

When choosing a name for their offspring, Nox don't really differentiate between sexes and simply choose something that sounds right to their ears. Surnames on the other hand are fairly important and unique to each family of Nox. If an individual meets another Nox with the same Surname, they can be fairly certain they are related in some way or another.

Nox names tend to sound clicky, crackly and harsh and can be quite hard to pronounce for foreign speakers.

Grotto Nox

As a Grotto Nox, you typically value tradition and controlled progress above everything. Your natural understanding of the arcane helps you greatly when trying to identify Spells or Enchantments and supports you with means for subtle communication which becomes essential when leaving the fortified cities.

Shared Mind. You know the Message Cantrip. Once you reach 3rd level, you can cast the Comprehend Languages Spell with this trait; you regain the ability to cast it when you finish a long rest. Once you reach 5th level, you can also cast the Detect Thoughts spell once per day with this trait; you regain the ability to cast it when you finish a long rest. Your Wisdom/Intelligence/Charisma is your spellcasting ability for these spells.

Infusion Expert. Whenever you make an Intelligence (Arcana) check related to magical Enchantments, you are considered proficient in the Arcana skill and add double your proficiency bonus to the check, instead of your normal proficiency bonus.

Forest Nox

As a Forest Nox, your kind chose to leave the depths of the Underdark generations ago. You are used to the thick, dark woods of the surface world and know exactly how to traverse them. Your body is far more used to physical strain because of your kind adapting to a life without arcane machinery and automation. You are also far more willing to adapt to new cultures or circumstances.

Ambush Hunter. You know the Primal Savagery Cantrip. Once your reach 3rd level, you can cast the Feather Fall Spell with this trait; you regain the ability to cast it when you finish a long rest. Once you reach 5th level, you can also cast the Pass without a trace spell once per day with this trait; you regain the ability to cast it when you finish a long rest. Your Wisdom/Intelligence/Charisma is your spellcasting ability for these spells.

Treetop Dweller.  You gain a climbing speed of 25 feet.
